Eukaryotic

Eukaryotic cells are complex, organized cells defined by a true, membrane-bound nucleus containing linear DNA. They have numerous membrane-bound organelles that perform specific functions: mitochondria generate energy (ATP), the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) produces proteins and lipids, the Golgi apparatus modifies and packages molecules, and lysosomes and peroxisomes handle waste processing and detoxification. Plant cells also feature chloroplasts for photosynthesis, a rigid cell wall for structure, and a large central vacuole for storage and pressure maintenance.



Eukaryotic cells are defined by their complex internal structure. A major feature is the nucleus, a membrane-bound sac containing linear DNA organized with proteins into chromosomes. Eukaryotic cells also have a cytoskeleton made of actin and tubulin filaments that shape the cell and assist in movement. They use specialized flagella (or undulipodia) with a unique "9+2" microtubule structure for locomotion. Their internal organization includes organelles like the endoplasmic reticulum for transport and production of lipids and proteins, Golgi bodies for packaging, mitochondria for energy production, and, in some cells, plastids for photosynthesis. Mitochondria and plastids even have their own DNA, reflecting their evolutionary origin from symbiotic bacteria.



Characteristics
Nutrition: Plant cells are autotrophic, meaning they produce their own food through photosynthesis using sunlight.

Metabolism: They convert glucose into usable energy through cellular processes.

Homeostasis: Vacuoles regulate water and nutrients, while chloroplasts provide energy to keep the cell balanced.

Reproduction: Plant cells can reproduce asexually by mitosis or sexually through meiosis.

Environment: They thrive in sunlit areas, on land or in water, where light is available for photosynthesis.

Identified Organelles and Descriptions

Nucleus: Stores DNA and controls the cell's activities.

Mitochondria: Converts sugar into energy the cell can use.

Ribosomes: Build proteins; found floating in the cytoplasm or on the rough ER.

Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Has ribosomes; helps make and transport proteins.

Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Makes fats and detoxifies harmful substances.

Golgi Apparatus: Sorts, packages, and delivers proteins and other materials.

Chloroplasts: Capture sunlight and turn it into food through photosynthesis.

Lysosomes: Break down waste and damaged parts inside the cell.

Plasma Membrane: Controls what enters and exits the cell.
